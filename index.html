Paris, France

Paris, France's capital, is a major European city and a global center for art, fashion, gastronomy and culture. Its picturesque 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine.

Beyond such landmarks as the Eiffel Tower and the 12th-century, Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral, the city is known for its cafe culture and designer boutiques along the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré.

Must-See Attractions

Eiffel Tower

The iconic iron tower offering breathtaking views of the city from its observation decks.

Louvre Museum

The world's largest art museum and home to the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo.

Notre-Dame Cathedral

A masterpiece of French Gothic architecture, dating back to the 12th century.

Montmartre

Historic artistic neighborhood with the Sacré-Cœur basilica and charming streets.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: April-June and September-October for pleasant weather
  • Public transportation (Metro) is efficient and covers the entire city
  • Learn a few basic French phrases - locals appreciate the effort
  • Purchase museum passes in advance to skip long lines

Kyoto, Japan

Kyoto, once the capital of Japan, is a city on the island of Honshu. It's famous for its numerous classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines and traditional wooden houses.

It's also known for formal traditions such as kaiseki dining, consisting of multiple courses of precise dishes, and geisha, female entertainers often found in the Gion district.

Must-See Attractions

Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)

A Zen temple with its top two floors completely covered in gold leaf.

Fushimi Inari Taisha

Famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates straddling a network of trails.

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

A mesmerizing forest of towering bamboo stalks creating a natural tunnel.

Gion District

Kyoto's most famous geisha district with preserved machiya houses.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: March-April for cherry blossoms or October-November for fall colors
  • Purchase a JR Pass if arriving from Tokyo for unlimited Shinkansen rides
  • Try traditional kaiseki ryori (multi-course meal) at a ryokan
  • Respect temple etiquette - remove shoes where required

Santorini, Greece

Santorini is one of the Cyclades islands in the Aegean Sea. It was devastated by a volcanic eruption in the 16th century BC, forever shaping its rugged landscape.

The whitewashed, cubiform houses of its 2 principal towns, Fira and Oia, cling to cliffs above an underwater caldera (crater). They overlook the sea, small islands to the west and beaches made up of black, red and white lava pebbles.

Must-See Attractions

Oia Village

Famous for its blue-domed churches, white cave houses, and spectacular sunsets.

Red Beach

Unique beach with red volcanic sand and towering crimson cliffs.

Ancient Thera

Ruins of an ancient city perched on a rocky promontory with panoramic views.

Volcano & Hot Springs

Take a boat tour to the volcanic islands and swim in the warm thermal waters.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: Late April to early June and September to October
  • July-August is peak season with crowds and high prices
  • Wear comfortable shoes - lots of steps and uneven paths
  • Try local specialties like fava, white eggplant, and Santorini tomatoes
